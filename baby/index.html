Per-Feed Amounts (8-16 weeks)

  • Daily total: 24-32 oz
  • Don't force — watch for fullness cues

🌙 Nighttime Guide (7:00 PM — 7:00 AM)

Full Overnight Timeline

  • 6:15 PM — Start bedtime routine (bath, lotion, pajamas, swaddle)
  • 6:45 PM — Final bottle: 4-6 oz. Dim room, quiet voice
  • 7:00 PM — In crib. Drowsy but awake. Sound machine on. Lights OFF. Walk out.
  • 7:00-7:15 PM — Baby may fuss/cry. Wait full 5 minutes before checking
  • 7:15 PM check (if needed) — No pick up. Shush, pat, replace pacifier. Walk out. Wait another 5 min.
  • 7:30 PM — Baby should be asleep. If not, repeat check cycle.
  • 10:00-11:00 PM — Optional dream feed: 3-4 oz without fully waking baby. Some parents skip this by 8 weeks. If baby sleeps through, don't wake for it.
  • 1:00-4:00 AM — Night wake:

When Baby Wakes at Night

  • Step 1: WAIT. Don't rush in. Give 5 full minutes — baby may resettle alone
  • Step 2: If still crying after 5 min — brief check. Shush, pat, pacifier. No lights. No talking. No eye contact. Don't pick up.
  • Step 3: If it's been 4+ hours since last feed, offer 4-5 oz. Keep lights OFF. No diaper change unless soiled. No interaction. Pure business.
  • Step 4: Feed done → back in crib immediately. Walk out.
  • Step 5: If baby cries again after feed, wait 5 min. They're learning to self-settle.

Night Feed Rules

  • Lights stay OFF — use dim nightlight only if you must see
  • No talking, no cooing, no smiling, no eye contact
  • No diaper change unless soiled or soaked through
  • Feed 4-5 oz max — don't overfeed at night
  • Total time out of crib: under 20 minutes
  • Goal by 12-16 weeks: zero night feeds

Common Night Wake Windows

  • 10-11 PM wake: Likely hunger if no dream feed. Offer 3-4 oz.
  • 1-2 AM wake: Could be hunger or habit. If last feed was 7 PM, offer feed. If dream feed was at 10 PM, wait 5 min first.
  • 3-4 AM wake: Most common single night feed time. Offer 4-5 oz, back down.
  • 4:30-5:30 AM wake: This is NOT morning. Keep dark. Offer pacifier. Do NOT start the day. Treat as nighttime until 7 AM.
  • 6:00-6:30 AM wake: Still not morning. Try to get baby back to sleep or at minimum keep in crib quietly until 7:00 AM.
  • 7:00 AM: THIS is morning. Lights on, voice up, start the day even if baby wants more sleep. The 7 AM anchor sets everything.

Troubleshooting Night Sleep

  • Waking every 2 hours? Feed-to-sleep association. Baby needs to learn to fall asleep without the bottle. Put down drowsy, not asleep.
  • Won't go back down after feed? You're making it too interesting. Darker, quieter, less interaction.
  • Fighting bedtime? Overtired. Move bedtime 15-30 min earlier.
  • Waking at same time every night but not hungry? Habitual wake. Don't feed — just check, pat, pacifier, leave.
  • Wide awake at 3 AM, happy? Don't engage. Dark room, boring. They'll learn night is for sleeping.

Safe Sleep (Every Night)

  • Always on back
  • Firm flat mattress, nothing else in crib
  • No blankets, pillows, toys, bumpers
  • Room temp 68-72°F
  • Swaddle snug at chest, loose at hips
  • Sound machine all night
☀️ Daytime Sleep Guide

Nap Rules (8-16 Weeks)

  • 4-5 naps/day (3 long + 1 catnap)
  • Wake windows: 1-2 hours max
  • Swaddle for every nap (arms in)
  • Dark room + white noise
  • Put down drowsy but awake
  • If crying, wait 5 min before checking
  • Check = shush, pat, pacifier. No picking up.
  • Cap naps at 2 hours to protect nighttime sleep
  • Last nap ends by 5:30-5:45 PM

Sleepy Cues — Act Fast

  • Yawning
  • Eye rubbing
  • Looking away
  • Fussing after being happy
  • Jerky movements
  • Overtired = harder to sleep. Don't miss the window.
🌙 Night Sleep Summary

Quick Night Rules

  • Bedtime: 7:00-7:30 PM — same every night
  • Full routine before bed (see Night tab)
  • Completely dark room, white noise all night
  • Night feed: lights off, silent, no eye contact
  • Goal: one feed max, working to zero
  • 7 AM is the anchor — wake baby even if sleeping
