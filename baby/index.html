🧭 Amounts for 8-16 weeks

  • Day feeds: 4-6 oz every ~3 hours, usually 5 daytime feeds.
  • Night feed: 4-5 oz only if baby truly wakes and needs it.
  • Daily target: about 24-32 oz total. Follow cues instead of forcing the bottle.

✅ After feeding

  • Burp thoroughly: shoulder or lap, gentle pats for 5-10 minutes.
  • Stay upright: hold upright 10-15 minutes to reduce spit-up and reflux.
  • Use wake time: tummy time, talking, and play after daytime feeds.

🚫 Right after a bottle

  • Do not lay flat immediately: reflux risk goes up fast.
  • Do not bounce or jostle: stomach needs a minute to settle.
  • Do not feed to sleep: keep the feed-wake-sleep rhythm intact.
Night
The full 7 PM to 7 AM guide: timeline, wake protocol, night feed rules, and fast troubleshooting.

🌙 Overnight timeline

6:15 PM
Bath + bedtime routine starts
Bath, lotion, pajamas, swaddle, and calm transition into the night.
6:45 PM
Final bottle
Offer 4-6 oz in a dim, quiet room. Keep the energy soft.
7:00 PM
In crib
Drowsy but awake. Sound machine on. Lights off. Let baby finish falling asleep in the crib.
10:00-10:30 PM
Optional dream feed
3-4 oz only if it helps the stretch. Keep baby very sleepy and do not fully wake.
Middle of night
If baby wakes
Wait 5 full minutes. If still upset, feed 4-5 oz in the dark with almost no interaction.
5:00-6:30 AM
Early wake is not morning
Keep the room dark, offer the pacifier, and avoid starting the day too early.
7:00 AM
Morning anchor
Lights on, diaper, feed, and start the daytime rhythm.

🛌 Wake protocol

1
Wait first. Give it a full 5 minutes. Babies often resettle on their own.
2
Brief reset only. Shush, pat, or replace the pacifier. Keep lights off and voices low.
3
Feed only when needed. If it has been long enough and baby is truly awake, offer 4-5 oz.
4
Back down fast. Quick burp, straight back into the crib, then leave.

📏 Night feed rules

Lights stay off.
Use only a dim nightlight if you need one.
No social interaction.
No smiling, chatting, or bright eye contact.
No diaper change unless needed.
Only if diaper is soiled or seriously soaked.
Keep it short.
Aim for under 20 minutes out of the crib.

🕓 Common wake windows

  • 10-11 PM: more likely hunger if there was no dream feed.
  • 1-2 AM: could be hunger or habit. Use the wait-first rule.
  • 3-4 AM: common single-feed window.
  • 4:30-6:30 AM: still not morning. Keep the room dark and boring.

🩺 Troubleshooting

  • Waking every 2 hours: usually a feed-to-sleep association or too much stimulation.
  • Fighting bedtime: likely overtired. Start wind-down 15-30 minutes earlier.
  • Won't go back down after a feed: too bright, too loud, or too much handling.
  • Happy and wide awake at 3 AM: stay boring. Dark room, no play, no morning energy.
Care
Diaper timing, putting baby down, after-feeding rules, and the sleep-technique reminders that matter every day.

🧷 Diaper guide

Daytime: change the diaper before the feed. It wakes baby up just enough for a better bottle and protects the feed-wake-sleep cycle.

Nighttime: check the diaper before the feed, but only change if it is clearly soiled or soaked. Never do a post-feed diaper change unless it is unavoidable.

1
Day change first. Fresh diaper, fresh start.
2
Feed. Take the bottle while baby is still alert.
3
Burp and upright hold. Give digestion a calm buffer.
4
Wake time, then nap. Keep the order predictable.

😴 Putting baby down

  • Drowsy but awake is still the goal. Let the crib be where sleep happens.
  • Hand on chest, then leave. Brief reassurance without becoming the sleep cue.
  • If fussing starts, wait five full minutes before going back in.

✅ Do this before sleep

  • Burp well. Trapped gas ruins naps and early-night sleep.
  • Hold upright 10-15 minutes. Especially important after fuller bottles.
  • Swaddle and sound machine ready. Set the sleep environment before baby hits the crib.
  • Keep the room dark and cool. Aim for 68-72°F with blackout conditions.

🚫 Don't do this

  • Don't let the bottle become sleep. Break the feed-to-sleep link early.
  • Don't lay flat immediately. Upright first, then crib.
  • Don't rock or bounce all the way to asleep. Calm, yes. Fully asleep, no.
  • Don't use swings or bouncers as sleep space. Keep sleep on a firm flat surface.

🧯 If baby won't sleep after a feed

  • Check whether burping was enough. A second burp often fixes it.
  • Check the wake window. Too much or too little awake time both backfire.
  • Re-swaddle, replace the pacifier, and keep stimulation near zero.
  • As a last resort, pick up to calm, then put back down before baby is fully asleep.

🛏️ Safe sleep reminders

  • Always on the back.
  • Firm flat mattress, nothing extra in the crib.
  • No blankets, pillows, toys, bumpers, or positioners.
  • Swaddle only until rolling starts, then transition to a sleep sack.
