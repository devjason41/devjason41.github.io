🌙 Nighttime · 7:00 PM — 7:00 AM

🍼 Daily Feeding Tracker

0 ozGoal: 24-32 oz
Feeding Guide

Amounts (8-16 weeks)

  • Day feeds: 4-6 oz every ~3 hours, 5 feeds/day
  • Night feed: 4-5 oz (if wakes)
  • Daily target: 24-32 oz total
  • Don't force baby to finish — watch for cues

After Feeding — Do's

  • Burp baby — hold upright on shoulder or sit on lap, gentle pats for 5-10 min
  • Keep upright 10-15 min after feed to reduce spit-up
  • Gentle activity — tummy time, play mat, talking
  • Watch for spit-up — have burp cloth ready

After Feeding — Don'ts

  • Don't lay flat immediately — increases reflux/spit-up risk
  • Don't bounce or jostle — stomach needs to settle
  • Don't put in swing/bouncer right away — the angle can increase gas
  • Don't feed to sleep — this is the #1 rule. Feed → wake → sleep, always
  • Don't skip the burp — trapped air = fussy baby later
🌙 Nighttime Guide · 7 PM — 7 AM

Overnight Timeline

  • 6:15 PM — Bath, lotion, pajamas, swaddle
  • 6:45 PM — Final bottle (4-6 oz). Dim room. Quiet voice.
  • 7:00 PM — In crib. Drowsy but awake. Sound machine. Lights OFF.
  • 7:00-7:15 PM — May fuss. Wait full 5 minutes.
  • 7:15 PM — If still crying: brief check. Shush, pat, pacifier. Leave.
  • 10:00-11:00 PM — Optional dream feed (3-4 oz, don't fully wake). Skip if sleeping through.
  • 2:00-4:00 AM — If wakes: wait 5 min → then feed 4-5 oz → back down.
  • 4:30-6:30 AM — Early wake: NOT morning. Keep dark. Pacifier. Wait for 7 AM.
  • 7:00 AM — Morning. Lights on. Start the day. Wake baby if sleeping.

When Baby Wakes at Night

  • Step 1: WAIT. Full 5 minutes. Baby may resettle alone.
  • Step 2: Brief check — shush, pat, pacifier. No lights. No talking. Don't pick up.
  • Step 3: If 4+ hours since last feed, offer 4-5 oz. Lights OFF. No interaction.
  • Step 4: Feed done → crib immediately. Walk out.
  • Step 5: If cries again, wait 5 min. They're learning.

Night Feed Rules

  • Lights stay OFF (dim nightlight only if needed)
  • No talking, cooing, smiling, or eye contact
  • No diaper change unless soiled or soaked
  • 4-5 oz max — don't overfeed at night
  • Total time out of crib: under 20 minutes
  • Goal by 12-16 weeks: zero night feeds

Common Wake Windows

  • 10-11 PM: Likely hunger if no dream feed
  • 1-2 AM: Hunger or habit. If last feed was 7 PM, feed. If dream feed was 10 PM, wait.
  • 3-4 AM: Most common single night feed time
  • 4:30-5:30 AM: NOT morning. Dark + pacifier. Wait for 7 AM.
  • 6:00-6:30 AM: Still not morning. Keep in crib until 7:00.

Troubleshooting

  • Waking every 2 hours? Feed-to-sleep association. Must learn to fall asleep without bottle.
  • Won't go back down? Too stimulating. Darker, quieter, less interaction.
  • Fighting bedtime? Likely overtired. Move bedtime 15-30 min earlier.
  • Same time every night but not hungry? Habitual. Don't feed — check, pat, leave.
  • Happy at 3 AM? Don't engage. Dark, boring. They'll learn.
🧷 Diaper Guide

When to Change — Before or After Feeding?

Daytime: Change BEFORE feeding. This wakes baby up enough to take a full feed. Changing after can disturb a sleepy baby and mess up the feed-wake-sleep cycle.

Nighttime: Change BEFORE feeding (only if needed). Check diaper first. If clean, skip it entirely. If soiled, change quickly in dim light, then feed. Never change after the night feed — it wakes baby up too much.

Daytime Sequence

1
Diaper change first — wakes baby, fresh start
2
Feed — full bottle, baby is alert
3
Burp — 5-10 min upright
4
Wake time — play, tummy time, interaction
5
Nap — watch for sleepy cues within 1-2 hrs

Nighttime Sequence

1
Check diaper — change only if soiled. Dim light. Silent.
2
Feed — 4-5 oz. Dark, silent, no eye contact.
3
Quick burp — 2-3 min max. Don't fully wake.
4
Back in crib — immediately. Walk out.
😴 Sleep Technique Guide

Putting Baby Down

  • Drowsy but awake — this is the most important skill to teach. Baby must learn to fall asleep in the crib, not in your arms.
  • Eyes should be heavy, body relaxed, but not fully asleep
  • Place in crib on back. Hand on chest for a moment. Then remove hand and step back.
  • If baby fusses: wait 5 full minutes before checking

Do's After Feeding (Before Sleep)

  • Burp thoroughly — trapped gas wakes baby up
  • Hold upright 10-15 min — prevents reflux
  • Swaddle snugly — arms in, snug chest, loose hips
  • Dim the lights gradually during feed if it's near nap/bedtime
  • Sound machine on before placing in crib
  • Room check: 68-72°F, blackout dark, nothing in crib

Don'ts After Feeding (Before Sleep)

  • ❌ Don't let baby fall asleep on the bottle — remove before fully asleep
  • ❌ Don't lay flat immediately — 10-15 min upright first
  • ❌ Don't rock/bounce to sleep — creates dependency
  • ❌ Don't use swing/bouncer as sleep surface
  • ❌ Don't rush to the crib — burp first, upright hold, then crib
  • ❌ Don't skip the swaddle — startle reflex wakes them

If Baby Won't Sleep After Feed

  • Check: Was baby burped enough? Try more burping.
  • Check: Is baby overtired? Watch wake windows (1-2 hrs max).
  • Check: Is baby undertired? Not enough activity during wake time.
  • Check: Room too bright? Any light leaks?
  • Check: Too warm/cold? Feel chest, not hands.
  • Try: Re-swaddle tighter. Replace pacifier. Pat rhythmically on side.
  • Last resort: Pick up, calm down, put back down drowsy. Repeat.

Safe Sleep Reminders

  • Always on back
  • Firm, flat mattress — nothing else in crib
  • No blankets, pillows, toys, bumpers, positioners
  • Swaddle until rolling (then transition to sleep sack)
  • Room temp 68-72°F
  • Fan in room reduces SIDS risk
