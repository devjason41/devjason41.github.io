Feeding Guide (8-16 Weeks)

Formula Amounts

  • 4-6 oz per feed, 5-6 feeds per day
  • Daily total target: 24-32 oz
  • Night feed (if needed): 4-5 oz
  • Don't force baby to finish — watch for cues
  • Pace feeding: hold bottle at slight angle, pause every 1-2 oz

Signs Baby Is Hungry

  • Rooting — turning head, opening mouth
  • Hands to mouth
  • Smacking lips, sucking motions
  • Fussing (crying is a late hunger cue)

Signs Baby Is Full

  • Turns head away from bottle
  • Closes mouth, pushes nipple out
  • Slows or stops sucking
  • Relaxed hands (fists open up)
Daytime Sleep Guide

☀️ Daytime Nap Rules (8-16 Weeks)

  • 4-5 naps per day (3 long + 1 catnap)
  • Wake windows: 1-2 hours max before next nap
  • Swaddle for every nap (arms in)
  • Dark room — blackout curtains or shades
  • White noise — consistent, not too loud
  • Put down drowsy but awake
  • If baby cries, wait 5 minutes before going in
  • When you go in: shush, pat, replace pacifier — no picking up
  • Cap each nap at 2 hours to protect nighttime sleep
  • Last nap (catnap) should end by 5:30-5:45 PM

Sleepy Cues — Watch For These

  • Yawning
  • Eye rubbing
  • Looking away / avoiding eye contact
  • Fussing after being content
  • Jerky arm/leg movements
  • Act fast: overtired babies fight sleep harder
Nighttime Sleep Guide

🌙 Night Sleep Rules (8-16 Weeks)

  • Bedtime: 7:00-7:30 PM — same time every night
  • Full bedtime routine before every night (see Bedtime tab)
  • Room should be completely dark
  • White noise all night — don't turn off until morning
  • Night feed (if needed): lights OFF, no talking, no eye contact, no diaper change unless soiled — pure business
  • Goal: one night feed max, working toward zero
  • If baby wakes and it's not feed time, wait 5 minutes
  • If still crying after 5 min: brief check — shush, pat, pacifier — leave
  • 7:00 AM is the anchor. Wake baby at 7:00 AM even if still sleeping. This sets the whole day.

Night Wake Troubleshooting

  • Waking every 2 hours? Likely a feed association — baby needs to learn to fall asleep without bottle
  • Waking at 4-5 AM? Common. Don't start the day. Keep dark, offer pacifier, treat as nighttime until 7 AM
  • Won't go back down after night feed? Feed may be too stimulating — keep it boring, dark, silent
  • Fighting bedtime? May be overtired — try moving bedtime 15-30 min earlier

Safe Sleep Reminders

  • Always on back
  • Firm, flat mattress
  • Nothing in crib — no blankets, pillows, toys, bumpers
  • Swaddle snug around chest, loose at hips
  • Room temp 68-72°F
